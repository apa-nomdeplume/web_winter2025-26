Characters: A Guide



Dragons:


Dragons are an ancient race of sentient creatures native to the land of Alagaësia. While most roam free and did not commonly associate with the civilizations of other races, a significant number were the mounts and life-long companions of the Dragon Riders.

There are very few dragons left in Alagaesia, due to the terrible genocide instigated by the Dragon Rider Galbatorix in his bid to become the all-powerful king of Alagaesia. However, unbeknownst to everyone, there were still eggs and free Eldunari in existence, hidden on the island of Vroengard, the ancient home of the Riders. The race of the dragons is now beginning to flourish yet again as Eragon and Saphira work with the elves and the other races to return the population of the dragons to their original numbers.



Notable Dragons:


There are four dragons that are featured on the covers of the four books in the Inheritance Cycle. The following is a quick description of each.



Saphira


Saphira is the sapphire steed of Eragon, and is currently the last female dragon of her race. Daughter of Vervada and Iormungr, she often refers to herself as the "daughter of the winds". She and Eragon spent the entirety of the Inheritance Cycle working to defeat Galbatorix, the evil king of Alagaësia. Eragon and Saphira continue on as the teachers of the next generation of Dragon Riders, leaving Alagaësia never to return again.



Thorn


Thorn is the ruby steed of Murtagh. He and Murtagh spent most of the series in Galbatorix's service (unwillingly), although they ended up helping Eragon and Saphira defeat him in the end of the series. They both went through many hardships while in Galbatorix's service; Thorn had a fear of small spaces for the longest time, although he managed to get over that fear during the happenings of the most recent sequel to the Inheritance Cycle, called Murtagh.



Glaedr


Glaedr is the golden steed of Oromis. He and Oromis were the last of the Old Ones, the elder Riders, most of whom perished during Galbatorix's struggle to reign Alagaësia. Glaedr and Oromis taught and mentored Eragon when he went to Ellesméra (the elven capitol) to learn and study. They were both killed in the battle at Gil'ead (a human city) by Thorn and Murtagh, although Glaedr's consciousness lives on in his Eldunarí, or his "heart of hearts".



Fírnen


Fírnen is the emerald steed of Arya. He hatched after the defeat of Galbatorix while Arya took him to Ellesméra. Fírnen mated with Saphira soon after the demise of Galbatorix, although that relationship soon ended when Saphira and Eragon left Alagaësia. Arya is now the elven queen, and Fírnen stays by her side.
